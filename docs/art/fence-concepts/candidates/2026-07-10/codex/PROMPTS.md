# Codex fence candidate prompts

These are the exact built-in image-generation prompts used for the two
non-production ADR-0076 calibration sheets in this directory.

## Wood kit

```text
Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: non-production calibration reference sheet for an isometric pixel-art tactics-game fence family
Primary request: create exactly two separate matching wooden fence components: (1) one modular low oak rail section with slender pickets and two horizontal rails, with no heavy endpoint posts attached; and (2) one stout matching terminal post with a restrained pyramidal cap and a small dark forged-iron band.
Input images: Image 1 is the wood material and palette reference; Image 2 is the existing terminal-post design reference. Preserve their warm muted oak, restrained detail, and practical medieval construction, while making the rail and post feel like one authored kit.
Scene/backdrop: perfectly flat solid #00ff00 chroma-key background for local background removal; no ground plane.
Style/medium: crisp deliberately low-resolution pixel art shown large for concept review; orthographic isometric three-quarter view matching a 2:1 board diamond.
Composition/framing: the two components isolated and clearly separated, fully visible with generous padding; rail on the left and post on the right; one rail and one post only.
Lighting/mood: soft top-left board lighting; readable silhouettes and limited value groups.
Production brief: the eventual native rail subject will span 48 pixels and stand about 16 pixels high; the eventual native post subject will occupy about 10 by 28 pixels. This image is a calibration reference, not those final native pixels.
Constraints: background must be one perfectly uniform color with no shadows, gradients, texture, reflections, floor plane, or lighting variation; do not use #00ff00 in either subject; crisp edges; no cast or contact shadows; no scenery; no grass; no stone; no labels; no text; no watermark; no extra objects.
```

## Stone kit

```text
Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: non-production calibration reference sheet for an isometric pixel-art tactics-game fence family
Primary request: create exactly two separate matching stone fence components: (1) one modular low fieldstone masonry wall section with a restrained cap course and no terminal pier attached; and (2) one stout matching square terminal pier topped by a clean beveled capstone.
Input images: Image 1 is the weathered gray fieldstone material reference; Image 2 is the existing terminal-pier design reference. Preserve their cool gray, muted tan variation, dark mortar joints, and practical medieval construction, while making the low wall and pier feel like one authored kit.
Scene/backdrop: perfectly flat solid #00ff00 chroma-key background for local background removal; no ground plane.
Style/medium: crisp deliberately low-resolution pixel art shown large for concept review; orthographic isometric three-quarter view matching a 2:1 board diamond.
Composition/framing: the two components isolated and clearly separated, fully visible with generous padding; low wall on the left and terminal pier on the right; one wall and one pier only.
Lighting/mood: soft top-left board lighting; readable silhouettes and limited value groups.
Production brief: the eventual native wall subject will span 48 pixels and stand about 14 pixels high; the eventual native pier subject will occupy about 18 by 24 pixels. This image is a calibration reference, not those final native pixels.
Constraints: background must be one perfectly uniform color with no shadows, gradients, texture, reflections, floor plane, or lighting variation; do not use #00ff00 in either subject; crisp edges; no cast or contact shadows; no scenery; no grass; no wood; no labels; no text; no watermark; no extra objects.
```
