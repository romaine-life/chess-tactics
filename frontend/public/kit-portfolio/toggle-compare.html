Chess Tactics · UI Kit · Method Comparison

Toggle · atoms vs frame-gen

Same widget, two build methods. The frame-gen sprite is one baked 152×48 PNG. The atom version is a 9-slice track assembled from one mirrored corner atom, with the label as live text and the knob as a sliding sprite. At 1× they're twins — the difference shows the moment you scale it or change what it says.

Read: at 1× they're equivalent, so for a truly fixed-size / fixed-content control the baked sprite is the simpler ship. But the atom track is the same art with two properties the sprite can't have — it stays crisp under the UI-scale setting, and it hosts arbitrary text (AUTO / MUTED / a value) while the knob still slides. The cost is one assembler script and a knob element. If there's any chance the toggle scales or ever says something other than ON/OFF, atoms are the safer build.
